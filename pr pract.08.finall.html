Types of Moons

A journey through the different faces of our celestial neighbor

New Moon

The moon is between Earth and Sun, making it invisible to us.

Crescent Moon

A thin silver sliver visible right after or before a New Moon.

Full Moon

The entire side facing Earth is fully illuminated by the Sun.

Blood Moon

Occurs during a total lunar eclipse, giving it a reddish hue.

Blue Moon

The rare occurrence of a second full moon in a single month.

Supermoon

When the moon is at its closest point to Earth during a full phase.
